Mezo Hackathon Winners — Competitive Landscape for mezoCircles

Synthesized from the official winners blog + YouTube demo-day transcript (pfJqVrGW_N0) + per-project repo deep-dives. 2026-05-20.

TL;DR — three things that matter

1. mezoCircles's "real Trove autopilot" is uncontested. StratumFi shipped the self-repaying-loan narrative but sidesteps Mezo's actual CDP — they use a synthetic bMUSD + protocol-owned Tigris LP, not BorrowerOperations.openTrove. Invoiced uses a literal mock MUSD with a hardcoded $50,000 BTC price. TrovePilot explicitly disclaims borrower automation. No winner does real-Trove + real-MUSD + auto-repay-from-yield as a closed loop.
2. The "ROSCA / tanda" framing this project's pre-refocus pitch used is alive and winning. KhipuVault won 1st (Financial Access) on cooperative savings. MezoLotto won Community Choice (Daily Bitcoin) on PoolTogether-style no-loss lottery. Both invoked tanda/ROSCA vocabulary on stage. The angle wasn't dead — it was just unshipped.
3. Working testnet demos > slides. The host (Andre) opened by praising the unusually high share of teams already deployed on testnet. Every roadmap had "audit" as Q1 2026. The bar is "actually works on Mezo testnet with verified contracts," not "polished."

Deep dive: the four projects that matter to mezoCircles

StratumFi — closest narrative competitor, weakest execution

OVERLAPS PARTIALLY

Ademola Adebowale · Community Choice, Advanced DeFi · github.com/Ghost-xDD/Stratum-FI

What they shipped: Deposit BTC → singleton VaultController pairs it with protocol-owned MUSD in a Tigris LP → mint bMUSD synthetic against the position → harvester bot claims LP fees → DebtManager decrements user debt. "Zero interest, zero liquidation risk" — because there's no real CDP to liquidate. They do not use BorrowerOperations. Frontend at stratumfi.xyz returns 404. Last meaningful commit Nov 3, 2025.

mezoCircles's differentiation:

  • "The honest version." Real Mezo Trove, real MUSD users can spend anywhere — not a wrapper IOU locked in one protocol's LP.
  • Yield-source diversification. StratumFi is 100% dependent on Tigris LP volume. mezoCircles can route auto-repay yield from MUSD Savings Vault + Boar Finance veBTC + other sources.
  • Per-user Trove isolation. Their pooled architecture socializes blow-ups; per-user vaults don't.

TrovePilot — sibling, not rival

DIFFERENT PROBLEM

Vitaliy R · 1st place, Advanced DeFi · github.com/VitalR/mezo-trovepilot

What they shipped: Protocol-side keeper toolkit. Automates liquidations (single + batch) and redemptions (hinted, with Tigris swap-redeem-rebalance arb loop). Single TrovePilotEngine.sol wrapper + TypeScript SDK + reference bots. Pass-through custody, off-chain logic, no on-chain registry. Active development — last push 2026-01-20. Deployed on Mezo testnet at 0x878a85eaaF24902fD6985d3CB2D51a299E33F43c.

Roadmap explicitly excludes: borrower-focused automation, yield strategies, per-user vaults, Stability Pool automation — i.e., everything mezoCircles does.

What mezoCircles should steal:

  • recipient-pattern: vault forwards all proceeds to caller-specified address. Clean.
  • Thin on-chain, fat off-chain SDK — easier to iterate, lighter audit surface.
  • Pair mezoCircles in the pitch as the "borrower-side complement to TrovePilot" — judges (Andre) explicitly thought in pairs.

Invoiced — claims the freelancer niche, hasn't actually shipped it

PARTIAL COVERAGE

Janice Gathoga, Tevin Isaac · 1st place, Daily Bitcoin · github.com/Invoiced-Mezo-Hackathon

What they shipped: Solid invoicing UI + Boar Network WebSocket for BTC payment detection. Live at binvoiced.xyz. The lending side is a mock: their MUSDToken is an ERC-20 they mint themselves with hardcoded $50,000 BTC price. Their config references BORROW_MANAGER=0x123... placeholders. The user must manually call depositCollateral → borrowMUSD in separate transactions. Last commit 2026-03-24.

The opening for mezoCircles: Ship a paymentReceived(address freelancer, uint256 btc) hook on MezoCirclesVault. Invoiced (or any payment processor) can call it to atomically route a received BTC payment into a real Mezo Trove and mint real MUSD. Turn the 1st-place winner into your upstream integration, not your competitor.

BTCShield — different layer of the stack

ADJACENT REFERRAL

Manan Singhal · 2nd place, Advanced DeFi · github.com/MananSinghal123/musd

What they shipped: A reversible-call-option marketplace where a "supporter" injects BTC into an unhealthy Trove in exchange for the right to take it over at maturity. Real working demo (option exercised on-chain, verified in MetaMask). Built on a fork of mezo-org/musd — they had to whitelist their ReversibleCallOptionManager in the protocol core to call privileged functions like TroveManager.increaseTroveColl. Deployed at 0x3B1817EFf525190cd4D395c2586BA55bd4702314.

Why mezoCircles can't absorb this directly: mezoCircles is a third-party wrapper on the canonical MUSD protocol. We don't have the privileged-caller status BTCShield's protocol fork gave itself. mezoCircles could build a wrapped imitation (rescuer sends MUSD → MezoCirclesVault calls repayMUSD → NFT ownership transfer), but it's a meaningful v2 feature, not a checkbox.

What the judges actually rewarded

1. Working testnet deployments

Andre opened the show praising the unusually high share of teams already on testnet with verified contracts. Slideware was rare and visibly penalized.

2. Mezo-native primitives

Repeated praise for "could only have been built on Mezo." MUSD as rail, Trove as collateral layer, Mezo Passport for onboarding, Tigris for yield.

3. One-click simplification

Both Judy and Andre called out projects that made a complex DeFi flow feel like one button. StratumFi's "Turbo mode," BitSpend's tap-to-pay, KhipuVault's group savings.

4. Familiar UX over novelty

CreatorBank explicitly beat more inventive projects because it "feels like YouTube." Familiar UI patterns won over cutting-edge mechanics.

5. Protocol-level + user-level pairing

Andre conceptually paired TrovePilot + BTCShield. Judges think in ecosystem completeness — they reward complementary pieces.

6. Real users were never shown

Diplomatically silent gap: no team showed real testnet users beyond demo accounts. Adoption metrics were absent.

mezoCircles's revised positioning

Pitch in one line: "The honest Trove autopilot. Real Mezo MUSD. Real per-user vault. Yield routes from real sources. No synthetic wrappers, no mock tokens."

This positioning is defensible against every analyzed competitor:

  • vs StratumFi — they sidestep Trove with synthetics; we are the real thing.
  • vs TrovePilot — we are the borrower-side complement, not a substitute.
  • vs Invoiced — we are the real-MUSD rails their mock-token vault never built. Offer to integrate.
  • vs BTCShield — different layer; MezoCirclesVault could opt-in to a rescuer marketplace someday.
  • vs everyone else — different ICPs.

Concrete features to adopt

  1. From TrovePilot: the pass-through-custody / recipient-pattern, plus a thin-on-chain + fat-off-chain SDK split for the auto-repay keeper.
  2. From StratumFi: the "Turbo mode" UX (one click, multiple yield destinations) — but ours uses real Mezo Savings Vault + Boar Finance veBTC, not a synthetic LP.
  3. From Invoiced: Boar Network WebSocket for real-time BTC arrival detection. Useful for a future "auto-deposit on incoming payment" mode.
  4. From CreatorBank: familiar-UX-over-novelty as a design principle. Ship a screen that looks like a savings account, not a DeFi terminal.
  5. From MezoLotto / KhipuVault: opt-in social layer on top of the vault — group-savings cohorts or no-loss lottery on accumulated yield. v2 feature, ROSCA framing already proven to win.
  6. From every winner: deploy on Mezo testnet with verified contracts before pitch day. Non-negotiable.

What to cut

  • Anything cross-chain (Wormhole NTT, Base off-ramp). No winner needed it. Mezo-native is rewarded.
  • "Locked 1% rate" copy. Already cut after the technical audit; reinforced here because StratumFi tried "zero interest, zero liquidation" and judges didn't reward the pitch — they rewarded the mechanic. Lead with the mechanic.
  • Account Abstraction / passkeys. Confirmed dead on Mezo. Use Mezo Passport like CreatorBank and Invoiced did.
  • The "freelancer rent money" framing. Invoiced owns that pitch. mezoCircles is the treasury layer behind any payment processor, not a freelancer-specific tool.
